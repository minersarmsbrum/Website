## Page 2 — Your Public Website

Your website has six main pages, each with a specific purpose.

---

### Home Page
This is the first thing visitors see. It features:
- A bold welcome section with your restaurant's name and tagline
- A scrolling message banner highlighting your best offerings
- A brief introduction to who you are
- A section for customer reviews

The goal of this page is to make a great first impression and direct visitors to explore more.

---

### Menu Page
Customers can browse your full menu here. The menu is split into two sections:
- **Café Menu** — your daytime food offering
- **Pub Menu** — your evening food and drinks

Within each section, customers can click through categories (like "Starters", "Mains", "Desserts") to browse items. Each item shows:
- The dish name
- A short description
- The price
- Dietary tags (vegetarian, spicy, signature dish, etc.)

**The menu is fully editable** — you can update prices, add new dishes, or remove items anytime from your staff portal.

---

### About Page
This page tells the story of The Miners Arms — who you are, your values, and your history. It includes a timeline of key moments in the restaurant's journey.

---

### Gallery Page
A visual showcase of your food, atmosphere, and space. Customers can:
- Scroll through a grid of photos
- Click any photo to view it full-size
- Navigate between photos using the keyboard or on-screen arrows

**You can add or remove photos** at any time from your staff portal.

---

### Contact Page
This page gives customers everything they need to get in touch or find you:
- Your address
- Opening hours
- Phone number
- An embedded map showing your location
- A contact form (when a customer fills this in, you get an email alert)

---

### Reservations Page
Customers can book a table directly from your website. The booking form asks for:
- Name
- Email address
- Phone number
- Date and time of visit
- Number of guests
- Any special notes (allergies, occasions, etc.)

Once they submit, two things happen automatically:
1. The customer receives a confirmation email
2. You receive an instant alert on Telegram (your phone)

The booking also appears in your staff portal straight away.

## Page 3 — Your Admin & Staff Portal

The portal is the behind-the-scenes control centre for your restaurant. It is password-protected and only accessible to authorised staff.

### How to Log In

Go to your website address and add `/login` at the end.
> Example: `yourwebsite.com/login`

Enter your username and password. There are two levels of access:

| Role | What They Can Do |
|---|---|
| **Admin** | Full access — manage bookings, gallery, menu, and view all stats |
| **Staff** | View-only — see today's bookings and confirmed reservations |

---

### What the Admin Dashboard Shows

When you log in as Admin, you see a dashboard with:
- **Total bookings** received
- **Confirmed bookings** count
- **Cancelled bookings** count
- **Number of photos** in your gallery
- **Number of menu categories**
- A table of recent bookings
- Quick links to manage bookings, gallery, and menu

---

### Managing Bookings

Under **Bookings**, you can:
- See every reservation (past, present, upcoming)
- Filter by date or status (confirmed, pending, cancelled)
- Add a new booking manually (for phone-in reservations)
- Edit an existing booking (change date, time, or guest count)
- Cancel a booking
- Delete a booking entirely

**Step-by-step — Adding a phone booking:**
1. Log in as Admin
2. Click "Bookings" in the menu
3. Click "Add Booking"
4. Fill in the customer's details and click Save
5. Done — the booking is now logged in the system

---

### Managing the Gallery

Under **Gallery**, you can:
- See all your current photos in a grid
- Upload new photos from your computer (up to 50 photos total)
- Add a description to each photo (for search engines and accessibility)
- Delete photos you no longer want

**Step-by-step — Uploading a new photo:**
1. Log in as Admin
2. Click "Gallery" in the menu
3. Click "Upload Photo"
4. Select the photo file from your computer
5. Add a short description (e.g. "Chicken tikka masala with naan")
6. Click Save
7. The photo immediately appears on the public website gallery

---

### Managing the Menu

Under **Menu**, you can:
- Edit any dish name, description, or price
- Add a new category (e.g. "Sunday Specials")
- Add a new dish to any category
- Remove dishes or entire categories
- Manage both the Café and Pub menus separately

**Step-by-step — Changing a price:**
1. Log in as Admin
2. Click "Menu" in the menu
3. Find the dish you want to update
4. Click the edit icon next to it
5. Change the price and click Save
6. The new price instantly shows on the public website

---

### What Staff Can See

Staff members (non-admin) log in to the same portal but see a simpler view:
- How many bookings are coming in today
- How many bookings are confirmed
- A list of all current bookings

They cannot make changes — only Admin accounts can add, edit, or delete anything.

## Page 4 — Automatic Notifications

Your website sends automatic messages so you never miss a booking or enquiry. No action needed from you — it all happens in the background.

---

### Email Confirmations to Guests

Every time someone makes a reservation, they automatically receive a confirmation email. The email includes:
- A thank-you message
- Their booking details (date, time, guest count)
- Your restaurant's contact information

If a booking is later confirmed or cancelled by your team, the guest receives an update email as well.

**You do not need to do anything for this** — it happens automatically.

---

### Telegram Alerts to You

You receive instant alerts on your phone via Telegram (a free messaging app) every time:
- A new booking is made via the website
- A customer submits the contact form

The booking alert shows you the customer's name, date, time, and number of guests — so you know immediately, even if you're not at a computer.

---

### Daily Morning Summary

Every morning at **8am**, you receive an automatic Telegram message summarising all of today's confirmed bookings. It shows:
- Each booking's time
- Customer name
- Number of guests
- Total confirmed bookings for the day

This gives you a clear picture of what to expect before the day begins.

---

### Contact Form Alerts

When a customer fills in the contact form on your website, you receive an email notification with:
- Their name
- Their email address
- Their message

This ensures you can follow up quickly without having to check the website.

## Page 5 — What Happens When a Customer Books

Here is the full journey from a customer's perspective to yours, explained step by step:

---

**Step 1 — Customer visits your website**
A potential guest finds your website and clicks on the "Reservations" page.

**Step 2 — Customer fills in the booking form**
They enter their name, email, phone number, preferred date and time, number of guests, and any notes.

**Step 3 — Customer clicks "Book Now"**
The website checks that all the details are correct (valid date, all fields filled in, etc.).

**Step 4 — The booking is saved**
Their reservation is instantly stored securely in your database (a secure digital filing cabinet). It appears in your staff portal within seconds.

**Step 5 — Customer receives a confirmation email**
An automatic email is sent to the customer's inbox thanking them for their booking and confirming the details.

**Step 6 — You receive a Telegram alert**
At the same time, your Telegram account receives a notification with the booking details.

**Step 7 — You manage the booking**
Log in to your portal anytime to view, confirm, or update the booking. The customer can be notified automatically if the status changes.

---

The whole process — from customer clicking "Book Now" to you receiving the alert — takes less than 10 seconds.
